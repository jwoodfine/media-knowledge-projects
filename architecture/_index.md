Architecture in the context of real estate investment covers the built environment
from vernacular traditions through contemporary design movements. Articles in this
category examine how architectural style, period, and design philosophy affect the
operational suitability, leasing efficiency, and long-term asset value of commercial
buildings. Covered design eras include classical, modernist, post-modernist, high-tech,
new urbanist, and contextual traditions, with particular attention to their presence
in the markets where direct-hold real estate investment is evaluated.
