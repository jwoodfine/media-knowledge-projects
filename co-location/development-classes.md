Woodfine direct-hold deployments fall into three classes: Professional Centres, Vertical Warehouses, and Parking Structures. Each class addresses a distinct occupier demand category and corresponds to a specific proximity relationship with the power centre anchor. The class determines the physical form — including structural system, floor plate geometry, and building services — while the [[co-location-strategy|co-location strategy]] determines the site.

## Professional Centres

Professional Centres are multi-storey office buildings that serve the five target tenant categories: academic, business, civic, laboratory, and medical. They are the primary and most widely deployed class, positioned within 1.0 kilometre of a [[power-centres|power centre]].

Professional Centre deployments include four sub-types based on proximity and market context:

**Professional Offices** are the baseline multi-storey office product. Each occupier category — academic, business, civic, lab, medical — receives a purpose-configured fit-out within the [[fixed-floor-plates|fixed floor plate]], with furniture and equipment geometry derived from the specific use case.

**Retail Select** positions single-storey commercial-retail uses (bank branches, drive-through services) within the 1.0-kilometre power centre radius. These serve as street-level complements to the multi-storey professional building in a [[topic-campus-design|campus deployment]].

**Suburban Office** places multi-storey office product within 2.0 kilometres of the power centre anchor, where site geometry or land availability prevents 1.0-kilometre positioning.

## Vertical Warehouses

Vertical Warehouses are intended as a purpose-built solution for inter-city fringe locations requiring vertical stacking of industrial and logistics functions. Unlike low-rise warehouse platforms, the Vertical Warehouse model is designed to address urban land scarcity while serving last-mile and regional logistics demand concentrated near population centres.

Vertical Warehouses are planned for locations beyond the power centre proximity criterion; their site selection is governed by inter-city logistics network topology rather than retail adjacency. The class is under development and is intended to expand the direct-hold portfolio beyond the office-focused Professional Centre model.

## Parking Structures

Parking Structures are intended for deployment at regional transit hubs and airport catchment zones, providing multi-storey structured parking infrastructure as a standalone direct-hold asset. The transit hub context means the occupancy driver is regional mobility infrastructure rather than retail anchor adjacency.

Parking Structures are planned as part of the Woodfine direct-hold portfolio expansion. The [[topic-co-location-methodology|co-location adjacency methodology]] does not apply to this class; site selection is governed by transit hub capacity and modal connectivity analysis.

## The rollout program as a cross-class framework

The [[topic-co-location-methodology|rollout program]] — the systematic repetition of a standardized design across multiple sites — applies to all three classes. Each class has its own prototype geometry and [[fixed-floor-plates|fixed floor plate]] standard, enabling consistent cost and schedule performance across deployments regardless of market or jurisdiction.

The class taxonomy is exhaustive and categorical. A deployment is a Professional Centre, a Vertical Warehouse, or a Parking Structure — no hybrid classifications exist within the direct-hold portfolio framework.
