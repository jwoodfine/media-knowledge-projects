The **Kelowna regional market** encompasses the city of Kelowna and its immediate satellite
communities — West Kelowna, Lake Country, and Peachland — in the Central Okanagan Regional
District of British Columbia. The Kelowna Census Metropolitan Area had a population of
approximately 222,000 in 2021, making it the second-largest urban area in the BC Interior
after Kamloops–Kelowna taken together. Kelowna is consistently identified as one of the
highest-income mid-sized markets in British Columbia and is the regional services hub for
the Okanagan Valley, drawing professional and retail demand from communities extending south
to Oliver and Osoyoos and north to Salmon Arm.

## Economic profile

Kelowna's economy is anchored by a diversified professional and services base with
agriculture (tree fruit, viticulture), technology, tourism, retirement, and healthcare as
primary sectors. Kelowna General Hospital is the largest employer and serves as the
tertiary care referral centre for the Interior Health Authority, generating consistent
specialist medical, legal, financial, and administrative demand.

The technology sector has expanded significantly since 2015, with a cluster of software and
remote-work companies attracted by the quality-of-life profile, lower real estate costs
relative to Metro Vancouver, and the presence of the University of British Columbia Okanagan
campus. This technology employment layer supplements the traditional professional service
base with higher-income workers whose service consumption reinforces the income signal.

Kelowna benefits from a substantial retirement and semi-retirement population whose asset
management, estate planning, medical services, and lifestyle consumption demands anchor
professional service tenancy. The combination of active retirees, technology workers, and
healthcare professionals gives Kelowna an unusually deep multi-layer demand profile for a
market of its size.

## Commercial real estate characteristics

The primary power centre node in Kelowna is concentrated along Harvey Avenue (Highway 97)
in the city's central retail corridor and the Orchard Park area, with Costco, Home Depot,
Walmart, and Canadian Tire anchoring the large-format retail base. The Orchard Park Shopping
Centre is the dominant enclosed mall for the Okanagan region, drawing shoppers from the
entire valley.

A secondary commercial node has developed in West Kelowna along Westside Road near the
Bear Creek area, serving the residential growth on the western shore of Okanagan Lake.
Lake Country to the north is developing commercial infrastructure ahead of continuing
residential growth along the Highway 97 corridor between Kelowna and Vernon.

Office supply in the Kelowna market includes both downtown professional space along Bernard
Avenue and the Pandosy Village node, as well as suburban professional parks in the mission
and south Kelowna areas. Medical and dental professional suites are concentrated in clusters
near the hospital and in established professional parks.

## Regional draw and catchment

Kelowna's commercial catchment extends significantly beyond the CMA boundary. The absence
of comparable large-format retail and professional services between Kelowna and Penticton
(to the south), Vernon (to the north), and the Shuswap (to the northeast) means that
Kelowna serves as the default destination for higher-order goods and services across a
catchment of approximately 350,000–400,000 people.

This extended catchment gives Kelowna commercial fundamentals stronger than its resident
population would suggest, and makes it particularly resilient to individual sector downturns
in the local economy.
