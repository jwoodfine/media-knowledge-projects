The **TOP600 Europe** index identifies up to 600 regional commercial real estate markets
across European countries meeting the population, income, and retail infrastructure thresholds
established by the [[regional-markets|co-location regional market]] framework. European markets
present a distinct set of structural characteristics relative to North American comparables:
denser historic urban cores, stronger public-transport dependence, more regulated retail
planning environments, and a broader range of income levels across member states of the
European Union. The index applies consistent screening criteria while accommodating these
regional differences in the ranking methodology.

## Scope and classification

A European regional market qualifies for inclusion when it meets the same three primary screens
applied to North American markets: population range, disposable income signal, and power centre
investment confirmation. In the European context, these screens are applied with market-specific
adjustments:

**Population range.** Cities and agglomerations typically between 50,000 and 500,000. In
higher-density European countries such as Germany and the Netherlands, the lower bound is
effective at 50,000; in larger-territory countries such as France and Spain, metropolitan
boundaries may extend across broader geographic areas.

**Disposable income signal.** The European income signal is assessed relative to OECD
household disposable income benchmarks for the relevant country, not against a single
continental standard. A regional market in Germany at the 50th income percentile may meet the
threshold where an equivalent percentile market in an EU accession state may not.

**Retail infrastructure.** European retail formats differ from North American equivalents.
The presence of pan-European retail anchors — warehouse clubs, hypermarkets, home-improvement
chains — serves as the infrastructure signal. Planning-restricted markets where national
retail penetration is constrained by zoning regulation are scored separately.

## Geographic coverage

Primary coverage focuses on markets across:

- **Germany** — the largest European national market, with strong representation in
  Baden-Württemberg, Bavaria, North Rhine-Westphalia, and the former East German states
- **Spain** — markets spanning Castile and León, Aragon, the Valencian Community, Andalusia,
  and the Basque Country beyond the primary Madrid and Barcelona metros
- **United Kingdom** — markets outside London and the South East, concentrated in the
  Midlands, the North of England, Scotland, and Wales
- **France** — markets outside the Île-de-France region, with coverage in Occitanie,
  Nouvelle-Aquitaine, Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, and the Grand Est
- **Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, Switzerland** — targeted coverage of OECD-standard
  income regional markets
