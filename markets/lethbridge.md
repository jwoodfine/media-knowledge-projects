The **Lethbridge regional market** is a mid-sized commercial centre in southern Alberta,
located approximately 215 kilometres south of Calgary at the confluence of the Oldman and
St. Mary rivers. The City of Lethbridge had a population of approximately 101,000 in 2021,
making it Alberta's third-largest city and the dominant urban centre for a regional catchment
of approximately 200,000 people extending across southern Alberta, the Crowsnest Pass, and
into adjacent areas of southwestern Saskatchewan.

## Economic profile

Lethbridge's economy combines a stable institutional and public sector base with agriculture,
healthcare, higher education, and retail services. The University of Lethbridge and Lethbridge
College together represent one of the largest combined higher-education enrollments relative
to population among Canadian cities of comparable size, generating student and academic
professional demand for services. Chinook Regional Hospital serves as the tertiary health
facility for southern Alberta south of Calgary, anchoring medical professional demand.

Alberta's energy-sector income, distributed to Lethbridge through oil and gas employment in
the regional agricultural and petrochemical sector, supports household incomes that
consistently exceed national median levels. Lethbridge has historically maintained stronger
retail sales per capita than its population would predict, driven by the extended catchment
served from the city.

## Commercial real estate characteristics

Lethbridge's primary power centre node is concentrated on the northwest side of the city
along Mayor Magrath Drive North and the Crossings shopping node, which includes Costco,
Home Depot, Walmart Supercentre, Canadian Tire, Staples, and associated mid-box retailers.
This concentration of national retail anchors confirms the income and demand conditions
required for professional centre viability.

West Lethbridge, the city's fastest-growing residential area, is generating secondary
commercial demand as the population west of the Oldman River canyon grows. The University
Drive corridor in south Lethbridge serves student-oriented retail and services demand
adjacent to the University of Lethbridge campus.

Professional office supply in Lethbridge is modest by comparison with larger Alberta cities.
The downtown core retains legal, accounting, and government-adjacent professional services.
The shortage of modern Class A suburban professional space represents both a market gap and
an opportunity for purpose-built professional centre development.

## Regional catchment

Lethbridge serves as the primary commercial hub for a large territory with limited
alternative retail destinations. Communities from Fort Macleod (to the north), Cardston,
Waterton, and Raymond (to the south), Taber and Medicine Hat corridor communities (to the
east), and Pincher Creek (to the west) depend on Lethbridge for larger-format retail,
specialist medical services, and professional services. This extended catchment of
approximately 200,000 makes Lethbridge commercial fundamentals robust for a city of 101,000.
