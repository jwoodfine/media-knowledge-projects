The **Inverness regional market** is the administrative capital of the Scottish Highlands
Council area and the primary urban centre for the northern half of Scotland. The Inverness
urban area had a population of approximately 48,000 in 2021, but as the only city of any
scale in the Scottish Highlands, it serves as the commercial, healthcare, educational, and
professional services hub for an extremely large geographic catchment of approximately
250,000 people across the Highland and Moray council areas. Inverness has been one of the
fastest-growing cities in Scotland since 2000, driven by renewable energy investment,
population migration from rural Highland communities, and the expansion of Highland-based
service industries.

## Economic profile

Inverness's economy is anchored by public sector services, healthcare, energy, tourism, and
distribution. NHS Highland operates the Raigmore Hospital in Inverness as the primary
acute care facility for the Highlands — the only major district general hospital between
Inverness and Shetland to the north — making Raigmore a Level 1 specialist hub for a
catchment covering thousands of square kilometres. The NHS is the single largest employer
in the Highland economy and the primary demand driver for specialist professional services
(medical legal, healthcare administration, specialist nursing professional services).

The renewable energy sector has become a significant economic driver. The Highlands and
Islands Development area holds the majority of Scotland's onshore wind generating capacity,
and Inverness serves as the headquarters and logistics base for a growing number of wind
energy developers, operators, and supply chain companies. Offshore wind development in
the Moray Firth and Pentland Firth areas has attracted further energy sector professional
employment to the city.

The University of the Highlands and Islands (UHI), a federated university model distributed
across the Highlands, has its primary administrative centre in Inverness, and the
Inverness College UHI campus is the main further and higher education hub for the region.

## Commercial real estate characteristics

Inverness's retail park infrastructure is concentrated at the Eastfield Way / Inverness
Retail and Business Park to the east of the city, which includes Marks & Spencer, Next,
Dunelm, Home Bargains, and a B&Q as anchor tenants, alongside food and beverage operators.
The Eastgate Shopping Centre in the city centre is the primary enclosed retail mall for
the Highlands.

The scale of investment at the Inverness Retail Park — comparable in anchor quality to
parks serving cities three to four times Inverness's size — reflects the extended catchment
and the absence of competing retail destinations across the Highland catchment area.

Professional office supply in Inverness is limited compared with comparable-size English
towns. The Crown area (east of the city centre) and the Beechwood Business Park are the
primary locations for professional services office space. The scarcity of Class A office
space is a distinctive characteristic of the Inverness market and represents both a
gap and an opportunity.

## Extended catchment premium

The Inverness catchment's geographic scope is the defining commercial characteristic of
this market: it is the largest geographic catchment of any market in this index relative
to its urban population. A professional services centre in Inverness captures demand
from the entirety of the Highland and Moray council areas — a territory larger than Belgium —
with no competing professional destination within 150 km in any direction.
