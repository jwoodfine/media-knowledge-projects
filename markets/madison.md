The **Madison regional market** encompasses the City of Madison and surrounding Dane County
communities in south-central Wisconsin. The Madison Metropolitan Statistical Area — Dane
County — had a population of approximately 680,000 in 2021, making it Wisconsin's second-
largest metropolitan area after Milwaukee. Madison combines two unusually powerful
institutional anchors — the University of Wisconsin–Madison and the State of Wisconsin
government — with a biomedical research industry, technology sector, and professional
services market that gives the city one of the highest median household incomes of any
mid-sized Midwestern metropolitan area.

## Economic profile

The University of Wisconsin–Madison is one of the top research universities in the United
States, with over 8,000 faculty and staff and approximately 47,000 students. The university's
research enterprise — particularly in biomedical sciences, engineering, and agriculture —
has spawned a substantial life sciences and technology cluster in the Madison metropolitan
area. UW Health, the academic medical network affiliated with the university, is the region's
dominant healthcare system and operates the only Level I trauma centre between Milwaukee
and the Twin Cities.

State of Wisconsin government employment adds a large and stable public sector workforce
concentrated in downtown Madison, with associated legal, financial, lobbying, and consulting
professional service demand that complements the university's research and student services
demands.

The technology sector — including companies in software, financial technology, and life
sciences — has grown substantially, attracted by the university talent pipeline. Exact Sciences,
Epic Systems (headquartered in nearby Verona), and a cluster of health informatics companies
have made Madison a recognised centre for healthcare technology.

## Commercial real estate characteristics

Madison's commercial geography is shaped by its isthmus location between Lake Mendota and
Lake Monona. The primary suburban commercial development has occurred to the west and east
of the isthmus.

The west side of Madison — particularly along the Gammon Road and West Towne Mall corridor
— hosts the primary power centre retail concentration, including Costco, Home Depot,
Target, and Walmart on the beltline (US Highway 12/18). East Towne Mall and the East
Washington Avenue corridor serve the eastern residential catchment with a similar retail
format concentration.

The downtown Madison commercial market functions as a high-rent professional services and
retail centre, anchored by the Capitol Square and State Street corridor. Law firms,
lobbying organisations, financial services companies with state government client bases,
and university-adjacent professional services maintain downtown office presence.

Suburban office development is concentrated in the West Towne area and along University
Avenue near the university campus, as well as in the Fitchburg and Middleton communities
to the west.

## Regional draw

Madison serves as the professional services hub for south-central Wisconsin and draws
medical referrals from communities throughout Wisconsin. The absence of comparable
specialist medical services between Madison and Milwaukee (130 km to the east) makes
UW Health the de facto referral centre for a large territory.
