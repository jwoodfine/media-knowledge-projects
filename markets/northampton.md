The **Northampton regional market** is the county town of Northamptonshire in the East
Midlands of England, located approximately 110 kilometres north of London on the M1
motorway. The Northampton urban area had a population of approximately 220,000 in 2021,
and the broader Northamptonshire county — encompassing Northampton, Kettering, Corby, and
Wellingborough — has a combined population of approximately 750,000. Northampton is one of
the largest English market towns and one of the few UK population centres of its size that
lacks city status, a characteristic that belies the scale and diversity of its commercial
and professional services economy.

## Economic profile

Northampton's economy has historically been anchored by footwear manufacturing (the town
was England's traditional shoe-making capital), logistics, and distribution, and this
logistics emphasis has grown substantially with the expansion of M1 corridor warehousing
and distribution capacity. Amazon, ASOS, Hermes Parcelnet, and a large number of major
retailers and e-commerce operators have established distribution centres in the
Northamptonshire logistics zone, making it one of the most significant distribution
employment concentrations in the UK outside the East Midlands conurbation.

The Northamptonshire financial and legal services sector is larger than many comparables
due to the county's proximity to London and its historical attraction of back-office
functions from London-headquartered financial and professional services firms. The county
council and the Northampton Borough Council (now West Northamptonshire Council) represent
public sector employment, although Northamptonshire County Council's financial crisis
(2018) introduced some public sector employment uncertainty.

Northampton General Hospital NHS Trust is the primary acute care hospital for
Northamptonshire and serves as a specialist referral centre for the county. The University
of Northampton, with approximately 14,000 students and a recent campus relocation to the
Northampton Waterside Campus (2018), has become a more visible institutional anchor.

## Commercial real estate characteristics

Northampton's primary retail park concentration is at the Riverside Retail Park and the
Nene Valley Retail Park along the A45 eastern corridor, where Marks & Spencer, Next,
Costco, Home Bargains, B&Q, and associated food retail anchor a comprehensive power centre-
equivalent retail strip. The Sixfields Leisure and Retail Park to the west provides
additional large-format capacity.

The Grosvenor Shopping Centre and the Market Square in the historic town centre anchor
traditional retail. The Northampton Professional Quarter and the Angel Street area retain
legal, financial, and insurance professional services tenancy.

The logistics economy has driven significant growth in industrial and warehouse professional
services (contract logistics specialists, freight forwarding, supply chain management
consultancies) that require office space near the distribution zones rather than in
the town centre.
