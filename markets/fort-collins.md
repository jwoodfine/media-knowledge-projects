The **Fort Collins regional market** encompasses the City of Fort Collins and adjacent
communities in Larimer County, Colorado, located approximately 100 kilometres north of
downtown Denver along the US 287 and Interstate 25 Front Range corridors. The Fort Collins
Metropolitan Statistical Area — which includes Larimer County — had a population of
approximately 360,000 in 2021. Fort Collins is the fourth-largest city in Colorado and
functions as the commercial and professional services centre for Northern Colorado, drawing
demand from a catchment extending from Loveland and Greeley to the southeast and into
Cheyenne, Wyoming, to the north.

## Economic profile

Colorado State University (CSU) is Fort Collins's largest employer with approximately
7,000 faculty and staff and over 30,000 students. CSU's presence anchors a technology
transfer and innovation economy that has attracted veterinary pharmaceutical companies, clean
technology firms, and agricultural sciences businesses to the region. Hewlett Packard
Enterprise maintains a major campus in Fort Collins, and the city has attracted technology
sector employers drawn by the university talent pipeline and quality-of-life profile.

The combination of university employment, technology sector, professional services for a
large agricultural hinterland, and a strong retail and hospitality sector gives Fort Collins
a high median household income that consistently exceeds the Colorado and US medians. The
healthcare sector — anchored by UCHealth Poudre Valley Hospital — provides both employment
and a driver of specialist professional service demand.

## Commercial real estate characteristics

Fort Collins's primary power centre concentration is on the south side of the city along
the South College Avenue corridor, where a continuous large-format retail strip extends from
the Fort Collins Marketplace to Harmony Road. This corridor includes Costco, Home Depot,
Lowe's, Walmart Supercenter, Target, Best Buy, and a comprehensive mid-box retail ecosystem
that confirms the income and demand conditions required for professional centre co-location.

The Foothills Mall, redeveloped as a lifestyle centre, anchors a secondary mixed-use retail
node in central Fort Collins. The Harmony Road and Harmony Technology Park area in south
Fort Collins has emerged as the primary suburban office and professional employment node,
housing technology firms, financial services providers, and medical professional groups
within a planned employment campus environment.

Old Town Fort Collins functions as the urban core, with professional office tenancy
concentrated in historic buildings along Mountain Avenue and the surrounding blocks. The
Old Town office market serves a different tenant mix — creative industries, boutique
professional services, and government — than the suburban professional park market.

## Northern Colorado regional draw

Fort Collins functions as the regional centre for Northern Colorado in categories where
communities to the south (Loveland, Berthoud) and east (Greeley) lack full-service
professional options. Specialist medical services, higher education, and certain retail
categories not fully represented in Loveland's more concentrated retail base draw
demand from a catchment substantially larger than the MSA population.
