The **Parking Ratio Standard** in the Woodfine Universal Governance Bylaws mandates a minimum of 3 parking stalls per 1,000 square feet of net leasable area for all office components of Woodfine Buildings, with a target of 4 stalls per 1,000 square feet. This standard is specified in the governance documents rather than determined at the site or project level because parking capacity is treated as a component of the **Building Experience** — a physical attribute of the asset that is engineered into the site configuration before construction begins.

## Origin: Institutional Retail Parking Design

The parking ratio discipline applied to Woodfine Buildings derives from institutional retail benchmarks. Major retail operators at the Primary Target level — the co-location anchors around which Woodfine Development Sites are selected — design their store footprints with specific parking-to-retail-area ratios calibrated to their peak consumer throughput requirements. These operators treat parking as a direct determinant of sales performance: inadequate parking constrains consumer arrival, regardless of the store's product offering or pricing.

The Woodfine Universal Governance Bylaws apply a parallel discipline to the professional office context. Woodfine Buildings co-located adjacent to Power Centres serve a tenant base — professional services, medical, legal, financial, academic — whose operations depend on reliable client and staff vehicle access. An office building that cannot provide adequate parking for its tenant mix at peak occupancy has a structural constraint on the professional services the tenant can deliver from the location.

## Minimum and Target Ratios

The 3:1000 minimum specifies the floor below which a Woodfine Building's parking allocation cannot fall. Sites where the physical footprint or municipal parking requirements cannot accommodate 3:1000 do not meet the Development Site criteria and are not advanced to the active pipeline.

The 4:1000 target reflects the parking allocation that supports full building occupancy at peak professional services demand — when tenants have clients visiting simultaneously with peak staff hours. The target ratio provides the margin that eliminates parking congestion as a constraint on building performance during peak occupancy periods.

## Parking as Building Experience

Woodfine's treatment of parking ratio as a governance-mandated specification rather than a site-by-site negotiation reflects the same logic that drives the Prototype's fixed Floor Plate geometry. A building whose parking allocation falls below the minimum threshold at any point in the asset's life — whether due to competing uses added to the site or municipal reallocation — fails to deliver the Building Experience that the Universal Governance Bylaws require.

By specifying the parking standard in the governance bylaws rather than in the construction drawings, the standard applies to the entire life of the asset, not only at the time of construction. A development partner or site operator cannot negotiate a parking reduction below the governance minimum as a condition of site assembly, because the minimum is a Universal Governance Bylaw requirement rather than a project-specific design choice.

## Regional Market Application

In Regional Markets, parking availability is a material factor in professional services tenant location decisions in a way it is not in dense urban environments where transit alternatives substitute for vehicle access. A Regional Market office building that does not meet institutional retail parking standards loses a portion of the professional services tenant base to alternative locations — typically suburban or highway-adjacent properties where parking is unconstrained.

The 4:1000 target ratio aligns Woodfine Buildings with the access expectations of the co-located retail anchor. A patient or client visiting a medical tenant in a Woodfine Building has the same parking experience as a consumer visiting the adjacent Power Centre — predictable, accessible, and without the congestion that constrains clinical throughput or client meeting schedules.
