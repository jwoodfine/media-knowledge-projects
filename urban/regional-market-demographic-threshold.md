A **Regional Market** in Woodfine Management Corp.'s development framework is a geography that meets a specific set of demographic and commercial conditions — conditions that distinguish it from both the major metropolitan markets that attract institutional capital in volume and the small communities that are served exclusively by local retail and services. The demographic threshold is not defined by population alone. It is defined by the commercial performance of the National Retailers that have already chosen to operate in the geography.

## The Primary Indicator: Retail Productivity

The most direct indicator of a qualifying Regional Market is the sales per square foot of the Walmart Supercentre operating in the geography. Sales per square foot is a composite indicator of trade area income, consumer visit frequency, and household purchasing capacity: it reflects what the surrounding population actually spends, not what a demographic survey projects they might spend.

A Walmart Supercentre achieving sales per square foot above the chain median is operating in a trade area where consumer purchasing volume exceeds the chain's average productivity threshold. This above-average performance has a structural explanation: either the local population has above-average purchasing capacity, or the site draws consumers from a wide secondary trade area — or both. Either condition indicates a Regional Market that supports commercial investment at a level above local-only service demand.

## Secondary Trade and the Regional Hub Characteristic

The Regional Market threshold is reinforced by the presence of strong secondary trade at the Primary Target. Secondary trade is purchasing activity generated by consumers who travel to the retail node from outside the immediately local community — shoppers who live in communities without a Supercentre of their own and travel to the node for major purchasing. High secondary trade at a Primary Target indicates that the node functions as a regional commercial hub, drawing from a catchment that extends beyond the immediately surrounding neighbourhood.

The regional hub characteristic is critical to the Woodfine development thesis because it determines the effective client catchment for professional services tenants. A Professional Centres building co-located with a primary target generating strong secondary trade will be accessible to professional services clients who drive to the node from secondary communities. The professional services tenancy at that location effectively serves not just the local population but the same wider catchment that generates secondary trade for the Primary Target.

This is the mechanism by which a Professional Centres development in a Regional Market of 30,000 to 80,000 population can support the same professional services operators — law firms, accounting practices, dental groups, medical specialists — who would typically seek leasehold space in cities of several hundred thousand people. The secondary trade catchment extends the effective population base of the Regional Market professional services economy well beyond its own resident count.

## The Role of Secondary Targets in Demographic Confirmation

The presence of Home Depot and Costco at the 1.0 km threshold adds a layer of demographic confirmation that the Primary Target sales figure alone does not capture. Home Depot's presence indicates that the trade area supports a trade contractor base that generates commercial construction and renovation activity. Costco's presence indicates that the trade area has a sufficient concentration of households with income and purchasing capacity to sustain membership warehouse patronage.

Both conditions are consistent with — and reinforcing of — the professional services tenancy thesis. A trade area that supports active residential and commercial construction (indicated by Home Depot) is a trade area where professional services operators in legal, accounting, and financial advisory fields have clients engaged in property transactions, business formation, and estate planning. A trade area that sustains a Costco membership base is a trade area where household income levels support discretionary professional services expenditure.

## What the Threshold Is Not

The Regional Market demographic threshold is a characterization of what the national retailers have already validated, not a set of inputs that Woodfine uses to replicate their site selection analysis. Woodfine does not conduct its own consumer demand forecasts, its own population growth projections, or its own assessment of income trends in candidate geographies. Those inputs are implicit in the sales per square foot of the Primary Target: if Walmart's Supercentre is achieving above-median productivity in a geography, Walmart has already validated that demand is sufficient.

The Woodfine development thesis is built on the premise that the most reliable indicator of a qualifying Regional Market is a National Retailer that has already invested at scale and is performing above its own productivity threshold. A geography that passes this test has been validated by an institution with decades of site selection experience and full access to proprietary consumer data. Woodfine's co-location methodology captures that validation rather than attempting to replicate it independently.
